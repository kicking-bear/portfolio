My story is influential to how I approach design


START

2013

I have always been interested in drawing, computers, games, and design. Ever since I was little, I was always making my own games and designing systems for my friends and cousins to play. If a game left out someone, or was too confusing to play, I would make up new games to make sure everyone could be included.

2016

Somewhere along the way, I convinced myself that I needed to do something traditional. I started working at a fancy law firm in NYC and quickly realized it was not for me. My days were slow, made even slower and tedious by the outdated software we were using.

2017

I had a thought, I could design this legal software better, clearer. I could create a better experience. Just like when I was younger. After some research I came across UX design.

My contract was ending and I was faced with a crossroad.

2017

I enrolled in a UX program at NYU. Now, my commutes to the city felt like they had purpose.

Making a transition like that can be scary. I had to work while in school and support myself but it was absolutely worth it. I felt like I was making my younger self proud.

2020

My time at the law office and my other degree proved useful too. A lot of what I learned in my sociology classes were applicable.

Now, I work at Martindale-Hubbell. Making and designing legal websites for attorneys and firms. Creating systems and modules for proprietary website builder, and even conducting UX research to make all of these things better.
